When money runs short at the end of the month, Betty has a quiet rule in her house. Andy eats first.

Andy is her dog, and he's also her company, her routine, and the reason she has someone to greet on the long days. Like many of the seniors we visit across Placer County, Betty would go without her own groceries before she would let him go hungry.

She shouldn't have to make that choice. That's what AniMeals is for.

"AniMeals has been incredibly helpful to our family and has made it much easier for us to care for Andy. We truly appreciate the support and assistance they provide."

Betty's family, Placer County

Through AniMeals, the same Meals on Wheels volunteers who bring Betty her meals also bring food for Andy. One stop at the door takes care of both of them, so Betty can keep the companion who keeps her going and still take care of herself.

Right now, AniMeals is in trouble. The program lost its funding, and without help it's at risk of running out. We have about a month, until July 15, to raise $15,000 so the bowls stay full for the 82 local seniors and the pets who keep them company.
